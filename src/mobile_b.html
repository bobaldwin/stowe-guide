On Foot

Hiking

Mansfield is the summit that earns the trip; the Pinnacle is the one you'll want to repeat. Four shorter outings fill the gaps.

Parking fills at the Long Trail and Sterling Pond lots by 8:30–9 am on fair-weather July days. Mansfield's ridge is exposed for a full mile — check the summit forecast, not the valley one, and be off the top before afternoon convection. Dogs on leash above treeline; the alpine tundra up there is a state-protected relic of the last ice age.

Mount Mansfield — the Chinvia the Long Trail · 4,393 ft · VT high point {{PIN}} Trailhead
4.6 mi RT | gain 2,800 ft | Strenuous | 4–5 hr | ★★★★★

The direct line from Route 108: relentless but efficient, gaining the ridge in 2.3 miles of rock staircase and low scrambles, then breaking out of the krummholz for the final open climb to the Chin. Views run from Camel's Hump to Whiteface in the Adirondacks, with Lake Champlain laid out west. Descend the way you came, or better, walk the ridge south to the Nose (adds ~2 mi of the best alpine walking in the state) before returning. Poles help on the way down; the mile below the ridge is steep, rooty, and slow when wet.

Best time · Trailhead by 8 am — clear ridge, open parking, and down before storms. Group call · the full group makes the Chin at a steady pace; the ridge extension is for whoever still has legs.
Alternative · Drive the historic Toll Road (opens 9 am, ~$40/car) to the Nose at 3,850 ft and walk the ridge to the Chin: 2.8 mi RT, ~600 ft of gain, all of it above treeline. The honest choice on a hazy-hot day, and nobody has to say the word "gondola." {{PIN}} Toll Rd
Stowe PinnacleUpper Hollow Rd · 2,651 ft {{PIN}} Trailhead
3.7 mi RT | gain 1,520 ft | Moderate+ | 2–3 hr | ★★★★★

The valley's benchmark hike and its best effort-to-view ratio: a steady woodland climb — one brief flat reprieve at the saddle — to a bare rock knob with the whole Worcester-to-Mansfield panorama. You'll share the summit; you won't care. The trail is well-graded but honest, with rock steps most of the upper half. From the saddle, the Skyline connector toward Hogback adds mileage for anyone who wants a longer day.

Best time · Before 9 am or after 4 pm — the summit ledge bakes at midday, and evening light on Mansfield across the valley is the photograph. Group call · everyone, no caveats. This is the hike to repeat on the last morning.
Sterling Pondfrom the top of Smugglers' Notch · 3,010 ft {{PIN}} Trailhead
2.3 mi RT | gain 850 ft | Moderate | 1.5–2.5 hr | ★★★★★

From the height of land in Smugglers' Notch, a short, steep, ledgy climb — rock steps, roots, a few hands-on moves — to Vermont's highest trout pond, ringed by spruce with Spruce Peak's slopes behind. Circle partway around to the beach-ish outlet for lunch. The drive up through the Notch's boulder narrows is half the event. Combine with Bingham Falls on the way back down.

Best time · Morning; the Notch lot is a zoo by 10 am. Group call · fine for all — short enough that the steepness never wears out its welcome.
Bingham FallsWest Branch gorge · off Rt 108 {{PIN}} Trailhead
0.5 mi RT | descent 150 ft | Easy* | 30–45 min | ★★★★★

A five-minute descent to a 25-foot plunge into a sculpted schist gorge — the valley's best quick payoff and its coldest legal thrill. The asterisk: the final drop to the pool is on worn, often-slick rock steps; good shoes, hands out of pockets. Go at golden hour when the tour traffic thins, or first thing with coffee.

Group call · Everyone to the overlook; the pool scramble is optional and the swim is a dare.
Moss Glen Fallsoff Rt 100 N · Stowe's tallest cascade {{PIN}} Trailhead
0.8 mi RT | gain 130 ft | Easy | 30–45 min | ★★★★★

A flat meadow walk, then a short climb beside a 125-foot ribbon of water threading a mossy cleft — quieter and stranger than Bingham, and somehow still under-visited. The viewpoint spur above the falls is loose; the base view is the better one anyway. Three minutes' drive from the Gold Brook covered bridge, which makes a natural pairing.

Best time · Morning light reaches into the glen; after rain it roars.
Wiessner WoodsStowe Land Trust · Edson Hill Rd {{PIN}} Trailhead
1.5–2 mi loops | gain ~200 ft | Easy | 45–75 min | ★★★★★

Eighty acres of hemlock and sugarbush laced with soft, needle-carpeted loops, kept by the Stowe Land Trust on land from the family of alpinist Fritz Wiessner. This is the recovery walk — the morning after Mansfield, the hour before dinner, the drizzle day. The Meadow Trail clearing frames Mansfield's profile; benches are placed where you'd want them.

Group call · All, always. No parking drama, no crowds, dogs welcome on leash.

Courts & Training

Pickleball & Fitness

Three ways to get a game, and where to swim laps or lift when the weather turns.

Where to Play

Topnotch Resort4000 Mountain Rd · indoor & outdoor courts {{PIN}} Map

The serious option. A nationally known racquet program that has embraced pickleball: pro-quality surfaces, round-robins, lessons, and ball machines. Non-guests welcome with a court fee, and the indoor courts make it the rain plan too. Reserve — call the Tennis Center 1–2 days ahead.

Stowe Parks & RecreationMemorial Park, School St · outdoor town courts {{PIN}} Map

Two minutes' walk from the village, free, first-come. The morning regulars (roughly 8–11 am) are friendly and will absorb visitors into rotation; bring paddles and a can of outdoor balls. Four of you constitutes your own rotation; six makes friends fast.

von Trapp Family Lodge700 Trapp Hill Rd · outdoor resort courts {{PIN}} Map

The scenery play: courts on the hill with meadow and mountain views, and the Bierhall a short walk downhill for afterward. Access comes with a fitness-center day pass; book via the concierge and confirm visitor court time when you do.

Gyms, Pools & Day Passes

The Swimming Hole75 Weeks Hill Rd, off the Rec Path {{PIN}} Map

Stowe's community nonprofit fitness center and easily the best all-around visit: a 25-yard lap pool with proper lanes, a leisure pool, a full weight floor, cardio deck, and drop-in classes. Day pass ~$25; check the lap-swim schedule online before you go.

PHIT PerformanceMountain Rd {{PIN}} Map

Training-gym atmosphere: racks, turf, kettlebells, programmed group sessions. The pick if your workout is strength work rather than machines. Drop-ins welcome.

Resort fitness centersTopnotch · von Trapp · Spruce Peak

Passes vary — ask; often bundled with spa or court access. Worth it mainly as an add-on: Topnotch's pass pairs with court time, von Trapp's with trails and the Bierhall, Spruce Peak's with a gondola morning.

Worth KnowingGetting a Game

July is peak season and Topnotch's indoor courts are the only guaranteed-dry pickleball in the valley — if a rain-day round-robin matters to the group, call before you arrive, not after the forecast turns.

Practical order of operations for a mixed day: town courts or Topnotch early, The Swimming Hole laps at midday when trails are hot, von Trapp late afternoon so the game ends where the lagers are.

Beyond the Trails

Outdoor Adventures

Flat water, high wires, mountain rides, and a fly rod in the Lamoille — the non-hiking half of the valley.
Gondola SkyRideStowe Mountain Resort, Rt 108 {{PIN}} Map
1–2 hr | minimal exertion | ~$40 | walk-up

Eight minutes to the Cliff House at 3,600 ft: ridge views without the 2,800-foot invoice. Best as a late-day add-on, or the storm-dodging way to show everyone the mountain.

Kayak / SUP — Waterbury Reservoirput-in at Waterbury Center State Park {{PIN}} Map
2–4 hr | moderate | $35–60 rental | reserve on July weekends

850 acres, most of the shoreline roadless state forest, water warm enough to swim from the boat. Paddle north from the Waterbury Center put-in into the quiet arms. The group outing with the highest approval floor.

River trips — Lamoille & Winooskivia Umiak {{PIN}} Umiak
2–3 hr | easy–moderate | ~$55–70 | reserve

Gentle current, shuttle included, farmland and heron scenery. The lazy-day alternative when the reservoir wind kicks up.

ArborTrek Canopy TourSmugglers' Notch (Jeffersonville) {{PIN}} Map
2.5–3 hr | moderate | ~$120–140 | reserve days ahead

A guided treetop circuit — ziplines, sky bridges, two rappels — through old hardwood forest on the far side of the Notch. Professionally run, genuinely fun, and the guides read a group's nerve well. Weight/mobility requirements posted online.

Disc golfBrewster Ridge & Fox Run, Smugglers' Notch Resort {{PIN}} Map
2–3 hr | moderate; hilly | ~$10–15 | usually walk-up

Two of the top-rated courses in the country, cut through Vermont hardwoods twenty-five minutes over the Notch. Brewster Ridge is the classic; rent discs at the welcome center.

Fly fishingLittle River, Lamoille, mountain brooks {{PIN}} Fly Rod Shop
half day | easy–moderate | guided ~$300+ | guides book out

Wild brook trout in the tumbling stuff, browns and rainbows in the Lamoille. The Fly Rod Shop on Rt 100 handles licenses, flies, and honest intel; July means early mornings and evening hatches.

Outfitter of RecordUmiak Outdoor Outfitters

The valley's paddling hub since 1988: rentals delivered to the reservoir, shuttle-included river trips, instruction, and the only reliable same-week availability in high summer. Their Waterbury Center outpost sits right at the reservoir day-use area — rent there and skip the car-topping entirely. They also run guided moonlight paddles in July — the sleeper pick of this section if the dates line up.

Morning water is glass; wind builds with the afternoon heat. Book the 9 am slot.
