The Friends' Field Guide

Stowe, Vermont

July 16–20, 2026 · Trails, lagers & long summer light in the Green Mountains
Welcome Cycling Hiking Pickleball Adventures Beer Food Quick Ref

Stowe compresses an unusual amount of terrain into one valley: New England's most storied mountain at the head of it, a village at the foot of it, and the Mountain Road running seven miles between them, strung with trailheads, breweries, and restaurants. Mid-July is arguably its best week — full green canopy, swimmable water, fifteen hours of daylight, and none of the ski-season crush.

This guide assumes you'll navigate by appetite rather than itinerary. Everything worth doing sits within twenty-five minutes of the village; most of it within ten. The one organizing fact to hold onto: Route 108 (the Mountain Road) runs northwest from the village past the resort and squeezes through Smugglers' Notch — a dramatic, guardrail-optional mountain pass — while Route 100 runs north–south through the village, connecting to I-89 at Waterbury, where the Alchemist's Heady Topper was born.

Ten Things Worth the Trip

  • Walk the Mansfield ridgeline — above treeline on Vermont's roof
  • Heady Topper at the source — the Alchemist, Stowe
  • Ride the full Recreation Path — then coast back to the village
  • Lagers on the Bierhall terrace — von Trapp, late afternoon
  • Stowe Pinnacle before 9 am — the valley's best effort-to-view ratio
  • Paddle Waterbury Reservoir — roadless shoreline, warm water
  • Sterling Pond — a trout pond hidden 900 ft above the Notch
  • Cady Hill's flow trails — why Stowe is a mountain-bike town
  • Dinner at Doc Ponds — the valley's best tap list, vinyl on the walls
  • Bingham Falls gorge — a ten-minute walk to the coldest swim in town
On Two Wheels

Cycling

One iconic greenway, two singletrack networks, and a 93-mile rail trail — all rideable from town.
Recreation PathVillage ↔ Top of the Path {{PIN}} Map
Paved | 10.6 mi RT | 1–1.5 hr | Easy | ★★★★★

The warm-up ride and the errand-runner: 5.3 paved miles climbing so gradually you barely register the 250 feet until you turn around and coast home. Ride it early — by 10 am on a July Saturday it carries strollers and dog walkers. It also happens to pass within striking distance of Idletyme and a short spur of Stowe Cider, which is not nothing. Swimming holes en route.

Cady Hill ForestStowe Trails Partnership · trailhead lot on Rt 108 {{PIN}} Map
Singletrack | ~11 mi network | 1.5–2.5 hr | Intermediate | ★★★★★

The marquee network, two minutes from the village — hand-built flow trails through hemlock, with the descent of Florence as the payoff lap everyone repeats. Climb Cady Hill Climb, descend Florence, repeat. Buy a day membership at the kiosk — it funds the builders.

Adams Campvia Ranch Camp, 311 Mountain Rd {{PIN}} Map
Singletrack | 8–14 mi | 2–3 hr | Interm.–Adv. | ★★★★★

Uphill and across the valley, Adams Camp rides bigger and quieter: longer climbs, machine-built berms, and a fast return. Start and end at Ranch Camp, the bike-shop-slash-taproom at the network's base — tacos and taps under one roof.

Lamoille Valley Rail TrailMorrisville ↔ Cambridge Junction {{PIN}} Map
Crushed stone | up to 26 mi RT | 2–3.5 hr | Easy | ★★★★★

The longest rail trail in New England passes through Morrisville, fifteen minutes north: dead flat, farm-valley scenery, river crossings, a covered-bridge turnaround at Cambridge Junction. A gravel or hybrid bike is ideal; there's a café stop in Johnson if you push on.

Rentals & Logistics

AJ's Ski & SportsVillage, on the Rec Path {{PIN}} Map

Path cruisers, e-bikes, hybrids — rolls straight onto the path; half- and full-day rates. Reserve on July weekends.

Ranch Camp311 Mountain Rd {{PIN}} Map

Full-suspension MTB demos with trail access out the door. Reserve 2–3 days out.

Pinnacle Ski & SportsMountain Rd {{PIN}} Map

Road and hybrid bikes; solid backup when AJ's fleet is out. Usually walk-in.

E-bikes are allowed on the Recreation Path (Class 1) and the rail trail, but not on Stowe Trails Partnership singletrack. Afternoon thunderstorms make morning the default ride slot; Cady Hill drains fast and is usually rideable a few hours after rain.
